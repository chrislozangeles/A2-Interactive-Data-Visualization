Subtheme: Speculation

This report explores speculation-related housing dynamics in Boston by using neighborhood-level trends in corporate ownership and owner occupancy (2004–2024), and connecting them to housing structure signals (vacancy / housing units per capita) and demographic composition (2024).

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How have corporate ownership and owner occupancy changed over time across Boston neighborhoods (2004–2024)? Which neighborhoods diverge most from the citywide trend?
  2. Which neighborhoods look like “hotspots” of speculative dynamics? (High corporate ownership levels in 2024 and large increases since 2004), and how do these relate to vacancy and housing units per capita?
  3. How does demographic composition correlate with corporate ownership in 2024? Are there visible patterns linking ownership structure with race/ethnicity shares and college housing presence?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Across 2004–2024, corporate ownership increases substantially while owner occupancy declines, with strong variation by neighborhood. A small set of neighborhoods appear as “hotspots” with both high 2024 levels and large increases since 2004, motivating deeper investigation into local drivers and housing structure context. In 2024, demographic composition and structural housing signals provide additional lenses that help describe where speculation-like dynamics may be most visible and which neighborhoods to prioritize for follow-up analysis.
